Why Rain Sounds Work (The Science of Pink Noise)

Not all noise is created equal. You may have heard of "White Noise" (like a static TV), but rain falls into a category called Pink Noise.

In Pink Noise, lower frequencies are louder than higher frequencies. This deep, consistent rumble mimics the natural rhythms of the human brain during slow-wave sleep. Research from the Journal of Theoretical Biology suggests that listening to Pink Noise can reduce brain wave complexity, effectively "slowing down" your mind and inducing stable sleep.

The Enemy of Sleep: Blue Light & Melatonin

Your brain has a tiny pineal gland responsible for producing Melatonin, the hormone that makes you sleepy. This gland is light-sensitive. When blue light (from TVs, phones, or even nightlights) hits your retina, melatonin production stops instantly.

This is why standard relaxation videos can fail. If the video is bright, it keeps you awake even while the music tries to relax you. Our Black Screen technology ensures that your room stays dark, signaling to your biological clock that it is time to shut down.
